Code of Conduct

This code of conduct outlines our expectations for members within this community. We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all and expect our code of conduct to be honored.

This community strives to:

    Be friendly and patient.
    Be welcoming: We strive to be a community that welcomes and supports people of all backgrounds and identities. This includes, but is not limited to members of any race, ethnicity, culture, national origin, colour, immigration status, social and economic class, educational level, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, age, size, family status, political belief, religion, and mental and physical ability.
    Be respectful: Not all of us will agree all the time, but disagreement is no excuse for poor behavior and poor manners. We might all experience some frustration now and then, but we cannot allow that frustration to turn into a personal attack. Diversity Statement

We encourage everyone to participate and are committed to building a community for all. Although we will fail at times, we seek to treat everyone both as fairly and equally as possible. Whenever a member made a mistake, we expect them to take responsibility for it. If someone has been harmed or offended, it is our responsibility to listen carefully and respectfully, and do our best to right the wrong.
Although this list cannot be exhaustive, we explicitly honor diversity in age, gender, gender identity or expression, culture, ethnicity, language, national origin, political beliefs, profession, race, religion, sexual orientation, socioeconomic status, and technical ability. We will not tolerate discrimination based on any of the protected characteristics above.

Unacceptable Behaviour

Any member must not engage in any intimidation, harassment, or abusive or discriminatory behaviour. The following point are considered to be unacceptable behavior

  • offensive verbal or written remarks related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race or religion

  • sexual or violent images in public spaces (including presentation slides)

  • deliberate intimidation

  • stalking

  • unwanted photography or recording

  • sustained disruption of meetups, events, etc

  • inappropriate physical contact

  • sexist, racist, or other exclusionary jokes

    • If You Witness or Are Subject to Unacceptable Behavior? (How to report)

    If you are subject to or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report to the community leader, here. All complaints made will remain confidential and be taken seriously. Additionally, the community leader will take care about the formal complaint against the person or the group of people and the Director of the college will be notifed at the earliest. In the context of in-person events, community board will also provide escorts as desired by the person experiencing distress.

    What will happen if people break the Code of Conduct

  • The individuals will be told to apologise

  • The individuals will be told to stop/modify their behaviour appropriately

  • The individuals will be warned that enforcement action may be taken if the behaviour continues

  • The individuals will be asked to immediately leave the venue and/or will be prohibited from continuing to attend any event

  • The incident will be reported to the College Authorities

  • The individuals will be banned for unforeseeable future
